### UN Staff Regulations and Rules

This section contains a structured, navigable version of the United Nations Staff Regulations and Rules.  
It focuses on the operative Regulations and Rules themselves and **excludes** high‑level overview, introductory material, and Charter text.

Use the links below to jump directly to specific Articles and their associated Chapters/Rules.

---

## Articles I–V

### Article I – Duties, Obligations and Privileges

Covers the fundamental duties, obligations and privileges of UN staff members, including:

- Status of staff
- Core values and general rights and obligations
- Honours, gifts and remuneration
- Conflicts of interest and outside activities
- Use of UN property and assets
- Performance of staff, information obligations and financial responsibility

[Open Article I – Duties, Obligations and Privileges](/policy/un-staff-rules/article-1)

### Article II – Classification of Posts and Staff

Defines the classification framework for posts and staff, including:

- Classification of posts
- Corresponding staff categories and levels

### Article IV – Appointment and Promotion

Sets out the framework for appointment and promotion, including:

- Letters of appointment and effective dates
- Nationality considerations
- Posts subject to local vs. international recruitment
- Geographical distribution
- Family relationships and change of duty station
- Inter‑organization movements and internal candidates
- Types of appointment (temporary, fixed‑term, continuing)
- Senior review and central review bodies
- Competitive examinations, reinstatement and re‑employment
- Medical examination

[Open Article IV – Appointment and Promotion](/policy/un-staff-rules/article-4)

---

### Article V – Annual and Special Leave

Governs hours of work, leave and related entitlements, including:

- Normal working hours, overtime and compensatory time off
- Night differential
- Official holidays
- Annual leave
- Home leave
- Special leave

[Open Article V – Annual and Special Leave](/policy/un-staff-rules/article-5)
